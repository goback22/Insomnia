margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">Franz Liszt was born to Anna Liszt (née Maria Anna Lager)<sup id="cite_ref-pfarre-paudorf.com_4-0" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-pfarre-paudorf.com-4" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[3]</a></sup>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_Liszt" title="Adam Liszt" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Adam Liszt</a>&nbsp;on 22 October 1811, in the village of&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raiding,_Austria" title="Raiding, Austria" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Doborján</a>&nbsp;(German: Raiding) in&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sopron_County" title="Sopron County" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Sopron County</a>, in the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary_(1526%E2%80%931867)" title="Kingdom of Hungary (1526–1867)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Kingdom of Hungary</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austrian_Empire" title="Austrian Empire" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Austrian Empire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-5" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[n 2]</a></sup>&nbsp;Liszt's father played the piano, violin, cello and guitar. He had been in the service of&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolaus_II,_Prince_Esterh%C3%A1zy" title="Nikolaus II, Prince Esterházy" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Prince Nikolaus II Esterházy</a>&nbsp;and knew&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Haydn" title="Joseph Haydn" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Haydn</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Nepomuk_Hummel" title="Johann Nepomuk Hummel" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Hummel</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Beethoven</a>&nbsp;personally. At age six, Franz began listening attentively to his father's piano playing. Adam began teaching him the piano at age seven, and Franz began composing in an elementary manner when he was eight. He appeared in concerts at&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sopron" title="Sopron" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Sopron</a>&nbsp;and Pressburg (<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Hungarian</a>: Pozsony, present-day&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Bratislava</a>, Slovakia) in October and November 1820 at age 9. After the concerts, a group of wealthy sponsors offered to finance Franz's musical education in Vienna.</p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">There Liszt received piano lessons from&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Czerny" title="Carl Czerny" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Carl Czerny</a>, who in his own youth had been a student of Beethoven and Hummel. He also received lessons in composition from&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinando_Paer" title="Ferdinando Paer" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Ferdinando Paer</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antonio_Salieri" title="Antonio Salieri" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Antonio Salieri</a>, who was then the music director of the Viennese court. Liszt's public debut in Vienna on 1 December 1822, at a concert at the "Landständischer Saal", was a great success. He was greeted in Austrian and Hungarian aristocratic circles and also met Beethoven and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Schubert" title="Franz Schubert" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Schubert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-6" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[n 3]</a></sup>&nbsp;In spring 1823, when his one-year leave of absence came to an end, Adam Liszt asked Prince Esterházy in vain for two more years. Adam Liszt therefore took his leave of the Prince's services. At the end of April 1823, the family returned to Hungary for the last time. At the end of May 1823, the family went to Vienna again.</p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">Towards the end of 1823 or early 1824, Liszt's first composition to be published, his&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variation_on_a_Waltz_by_Diabelli_(Liszt)" title="Variation on a Waltz by Diabelli (Liszt)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Variation on a Waltz by Diabelli</a>&nbsp;(now S.&nbsp;147), appeared as Variation 24 in Part II of&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaterl%C3%A4ndischer_K%C3%BCnstlerverein" title="Vaterländischer Künstlerverein" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Vaterländischer Künstlerverein</a></i>. This anthology, commissioned by&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anton_Diabelli" title="Anton Diabelli" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Anton Diabelli</a>, includes 50 variations on his waltz by 50 different composers (Part II), Part I being taken up by Beethoven's 33 variations on the same theme, which are now separately better known simply as his&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diabelli_Variations" title="Diabelli Variations" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Diabelli Variations</a></i>, Op.&nbsp;120. Liszt's inclusion in the Diabelli project—he was described in it as "an 11 year old boy, born in Hungary"—was almost certainly at the instigation of Czerny, his teacher and also a participant. Liszt was the only child composer in the anthology.</p><h3 style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial; font-weight: bold; margin: 0.3em 0px 0px; overflow: hidden; padding-top: 0.5em; padding-bottom: 0px; border-bottom: 0px; font-size: 1.2em; line-height: 1.6; font-family: sans-serif; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Adolescence_in_Paris">Adolescence in Paris</span><span class="mw-editsection" style="user-select: none; font-size: small; font-weight: normal; margin-left: 1em; vertical-align: baseline; line-height: 1em; white-space: nowrap; unicode-bidi: isolate;"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket" style="margin-right: 0.25em; color: rgb(84, 89, 93);">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Franz_Liszt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Adolescence in Paris" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket" style="margin-left: 0.25em; color: rgb(84, 89, 93);">]</span></span></h3><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">After his father's death in 1827, Liszt moved to Paris; for the next five years he was to live with his mother in a small apartment. He gave up touring. To earn money, Liszt gave lessons in piano playing and composition, often from early morning until late at night. His students were scattered across the city and he often had to cover long distances. Because of this, he kept uncertain hours and also took up smoking and drinking—all habits he would continue throughout his life.<sup id="cite_ref-seng1130_7-0" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-seng1130-7" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-8" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[5]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">The following year, he fell in love with one of his pupils, Caroline de Saint-Cricq, the daughter of&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_X_of_France" title="Charles X of France" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Charles X</a>'s minister of commerce,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_de_Saint-Cricq" title="Pierre de Saint-Cricq" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Pierre de Saint-Cricq</a>. Her father, however, insisted that the affair be broken off.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-9" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[6]</a></sup>&nbsp;Liszt fell very ill, to the extent that an obituary notice was printed in a Paris newspaper, and he underwent a long period of religious doubts and pessimism. He again stated a wish to join the Church but was dissuaded this time by his mother. He had many discussions with the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugues_Felicit%C3%A9_Robert_de_Lamennais" title="Hugues Felicité Robert de Lamennais" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Abbé de Lamennais</a>, who acted as his spiritual father, and also with&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chr%C3%A9tien_Urhan" title="Chrétien Urhan" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Chrétien Urhan</a>, a German-born violinist who introduced him to the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint-Simonianism" title="Saint-Simonianism" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Saint-Simonists</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-seng1130_7-1" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-seng1130-7" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[4]</a></sup>&nbsp;Urhan also wrote music that was anti-classical and highly subjective, with titles such as&nbsp;<i>Elle et moi, La Salvation angélique</i>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<i>Les Regrets</i>, and may have whetted the young Liszt's taste for musical romanticism. Equally important for Liszt was Urhan's earnest championship of Schubert, which may have stimulated his own lifelong devotion to that composer's music.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-10" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[7]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">During this period, Liszt read widely to overcome his lack of a general education, and he soon came into contact with many of the leading authors and artists of his day, including&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victor_Hugo" title="Victor Hugo" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Victor Hugo</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphonse_de_Lamartine" title="Alphonse de Lamartine" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Alphonse de Lamartine</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Heine" title="Heinrich Heine" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Heinrich Heine</a>. He composed practically nothing in these years. Nevertheless, the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/July_Revolution" title="July Revolution" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">July Revolution</a>&nbsp;of 1830 inspired him to sketch a Revolutionary Symphony based on the events of the "three glorious days," and he took a greater interest in events surrounding him. He met&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hector_Berlioz" title="Hector Berlioz" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Hector Berlioz</a>&nbsp;on 4 December 1830, the day before the premiere of the&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symphonie_fantastique" title="Symphonie fantastique" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Symphonie fantastique</a></i>. Berlioz's music made a strong impression on Liszt, especially later when he was writing for orchestra. He also inherited from Berlioz the diabolic quality of many of his works.<sup id="cite_ref-seng1130_7-2" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-seng1130-7" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[4]</a></sup></p><h3 style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); Paris in the 1830s had become the nexus for pianistic activities, with dozens of pianists dedicated to perfection at the keyboard. Some, such as&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigismond_Thalberg" title="Sigismond Thalberg" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Sigismond Thalberg</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Dreyschock" title="Alexander Dreyschock" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Alexander Dreyschock</a>, focused on specific aspects of technique (e.g. the "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-hand_effect" title="Three-hand effect" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">three-hand effect</a>" and octaves, respectively). While it has since been referred to as the "flying trapeze" school of piano playing, this generation also solved some of the most intractable problems of piano technique, raising the general level of performance to previously unimagined heights. Liszt's strength and ability to stand out in this company was in mastering all the aspects of piano technique cultivated singly and assiduously by his rivals.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-12" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[9]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">In 1833, he made transcriptions of several works by Berlioz, including the&nbsp;<i>Symphonie fantastique</i>. His chief motive in doing so, especially with the&nbsp;<i>Symphonie</i>, was to help the poverty-stricken Berlioz, whose symphony remained unknown and unpublished. On 9 May 1839, Liszt's and the countess's only son, Daniel, was born, but that autumn relations between them became strained. Liszt heard that plans for a&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beethoven_Monument_(Bonn)" title="Beethoven Monument (Bonn)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Beethoven monument in Bonn</a>&nbsp;were in danger of collapse for lack of funds, and pledged his support. Doing so meant returning to the life of a touring&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtuoso" title="Virtuoso" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">virtuoso</a>. The countess returned to Paris with the children, while Liszt gave six concerts in Vienna, then toured Hungary.<sup id="cite_ref-seng1130_7-5" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[14]</a></sup>&nbsp;He was seen as handsome<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-18" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-19" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-20" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[17]</a></sup>&nbsp;by many, with the German poet&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Heine" title="Heinrich Heine" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Heinrich Heine</a>&nbsp;writing concerning his showmanship during concerts: "How powerful, how shattering was his mere physical appearance".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-21" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[18]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">In 1841, Franz Liszt was admitted to the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freemasonry" title="Freemasonry" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Freemason's</a>&nbsp;lodge "Unity" "Zur Einigkeit", in Frankfurt am Main. He was promoted to the second degree and elected master as member of the lodge "Zur Eintracht", in Berlin. From 1845, he was also honorary member of the lodge "Modestia cum Libertate" at Zurich and 1870 of the lodge in Pest (Budapest-Hungary).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-22" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-23" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[20]</a></sup>&nbsp;After 1842, "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lisztomania" title="Lisztomania" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Lisztomania</a>"—coined by 19th-century German poet and Liszt's contemporary,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Heine" title="Heinrich Heine" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Heinrich Heine</a>—swept across Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-24" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[21]</a></sup>&nbsp;The reception that Liszt enjoyed as a result can be described only as hysterical. Women fought over his silk handkerchiefs and velvet gloves, which they ripped to shreds as souvenirs. This atmosphere was fuelled in great part by the artist's mesmeric personality and stage presence. Many witnesses later testified that Liszt's playing raised the mood of audiences to a level of mystical ecstasy.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-25" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[22]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">On 14 March 1842, Liszt received an&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honorary_doctorate" class="mw-redirect" title="Honorary doctorate" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">honorary doctorate</a>&nbsp;from the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_K%C3%B6nigsberg" title="University of Königsberg" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">University of Königsberg</a>—an honour unprecedented at the time, and an especially important one from the perspective of the German tradition. Liszt never used 'Dr. Liszt' or 'Dr. Franz Liszt' publicly.&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_Hiller" title="Ferdinand Hiller" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Ferdinand Hiller</a>, a rival of Liszt at the time, was allegedly highly jealous at the decision made by the university.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-26" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-27" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[24]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">Adding to his reputation was the fact that Liszt gave away much of his proceeds to charity and humanitarian causes in his whole life. In fact, Liszt had made so much money by his mid-forties that virtually all his performing fees after 1857 went to charity. While his work for the Beethoven monument and the Hungarian National School of Music are well known, he also gave generously to the building fund of&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cologne_Cathedral" title="Cologne Cathedral" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Cologne Cathedral</a>, the establishment of a&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gymnasium_(Germany)" title="Gymnasium (Germany)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Gymnasium</a></i>&nbsp;at&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dortmund" title="Dortmund" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Dortmund</a>, and the construction of the Leopold Church in Pest. There were also private donations to hospitals, schools and charitable organizations such as the Leipzig Musicians Pension Fund. When he found out about the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Fire_of_Hamburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Fire of Hamburg" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Great Fire of Hamburg</a>, which raged for three days during May 1842 and destroyed much of the city, he gave concerts in aid of the thousands of homeless there.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-28" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Weimar</a>, where he had been appointed&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kapellmeister" title="Kapellmeister" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Kapellmeister</a>&nbsp;Extraordinaire</i>&nbsp;in 1842, remaining there until 1861. During this period he acted as conductor at court concerts and on special occasions at the theatre. He gave lessons to a number of pianists, including the great virtuoso&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hans_von_B%C3%BClow" title="Hans von Bülow" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Hans von Bülow</a>, who married Liszt's daughter&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosima_Wagner" title="Cosima Wagner" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Cosima</a>&nbsp;in 1857 (years later, she would marry&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Wagner" title="Richard Wagner" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Richard Wagner</a>). He also wrote articles championing Berlioz and Wagner. After huge efforts and a monstrously intricate process, she was temporarily successful (September 1860). It was planned that the couple would marry in Rome, on 22 October 1861, Liszt's 50th birthday. Although Liszt arrived in Rome on 21 October, the marriage was made impossible by a letter that had arrived the previous day to the Pope himself. It appears that both her husband and the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsar_of_Russia" class="mw-redirect" title="Tsar of Russia" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Tsar of Russia</a>&nbsp;had managed to quash permission for the marriage at the Vatican. unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-Groveonline-33" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">The 1860s were a period of great sadness in Liszt's private life. On 13 December 1859, he lost his 20-year-old son Daniel, and, on 11 September 1862, his 26-year-old daughter Blandine also died. In letters to friends, Liszt afterwards announced that he would retreat to a solitary living. He found it at the monastery&nbsp;<i>Madonna del Rosario</i>, just outside Rome, where on 20 June 1863, he took up quarters in a small, spartan apartment. He had on 23 June 1857, already joined the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_Order_of_Saint_Francis" title="Third Order of Saint Francis" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Third Order of Saint Francis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-34" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[n 4]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">On 25 April 1865, he received the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonsure" title="Tonsure" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">tonsure</a>&nbsp;at the hands of Cardinal&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gustav_Adolf_Hohenlohe" class="mw-redirect" title="Gustav Adolf Hohenlohe" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Hohenlohe</a>. On 31 July 1865, he received the four minor orders of&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ostiarius" title="Ostiarius" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">porter</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lector" title="Lector" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">lector</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exorcist" title="Exorcist" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">exorcist</a>, and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acolyte" title="Acolyte" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">acolyte</a>. After this ordination, he was often called&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abb%C3%A9" title="Abbé" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Abbé</a></i>&nbsp;Liszt. On 14 August 1879, he was made an honorary&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canon_(priest)" title="Canon (priest)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">canon</a>&nbsp;of&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albano_Laziale" title="Albano Laziale" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Albano</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Groveonline_33-1" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-Groveonline-33" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[30]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">On some occasions, Liszt took part in Rome's musical life. On 26 March 1863, at a concert at the Palazzo Altieri, he directed a programme of sacred music. The "Seligkeiten" of his&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christus_(Liszt)" title="Christus (Liszt)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Christus-Oratorio</a></i>&nbsp;and his "Cantico del Sol di Francesco d'Assisi", as well as&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Haydn" title="Joseph Haydn" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Haydn's</a>&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Creation_(Haydn)" title="The Creation (Haydn)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Die Schöpfung</a></i>&nbsp;and works by&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach" title="Johann Sebastian Bach" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">J. S. Bach</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Beethoven</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niccol%C3%B2_Jommelli" title="Niccolò Jommelli" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Jommelli</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Felix_Mendelssohn" title="Felix Mendelssohn" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Mendelssohn</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giovanni_Pierluigi_da_Palestrina" title="Giovanni Pierluigi da Palestrina" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Palestrina</a>&nbsp;were performed. On 4 January 1866, Liszt directed the "Stabat mater" of his&nbsp;<i>Christus-Oratorio</i>, and, on 26 February 1866, his&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dante_Symphony" title="Dante Symphony" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Dante Symphony</a></i>. There were several further occasions of similar kind, but in comparison with the duration of Liszt's stay in Rome, they were exceptions.</p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">In 1866, Liszt composed the Hungarian coronation ceremony for&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Joseph" class="mw-redirect" title="Franz Joseph" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Franz Joseph</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Elisabeth of Bavaria" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Elisabeth of Bavaria</a>&nbsp;(Latin: Missa coronationalis). The Mass was first performed on 8 June 1867, at the coronation ceremony in the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matthias_Church" title="Matthias Church" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Matthias Church</a>&nbsp;by&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buda_Castle" title="Buda Castle" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Buda Castle</a>&nbsp;in a six-section form. After the first performance, the Offertory was added, and, two years later, the Gradual.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-35" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[31]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">Liszt was invited back to Weimar in 1869 to give master classes in piano playing. Two years later, he was asked to do the same in&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Budapest</a>&nbsp;at the Hungarian&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt_Academy_of_Music" title="Franz Liszt Academy of Music" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Music Academy</a>. From then until the end of his life, he made regular journeys between Rome, Weimar and Budapest, continuing what he called his "vie trifurquée" or threefold existence. It is estimated that Liszt travelled at least 4,000 miles a year during this period in his life—an exceptional figure given his advancing age and the rigors of road and rail in the 1870s.<sup id="cite_ref-wang214781_36-0" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; Liszt played&nbsp;<i>Au bord d'une source</i>&nbsp;from his&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ann%C3%A9es_de_p%C3%A8lerinage" title="Années de pèlerinage" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Années de pèlerinage</a></i>, as well as his arrangement of&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ave_Maria_(Schubert)" title="Ave Maria (Schubert)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Schubert's&nbsp;<i>Ave Maria</i></a>&nbsp;for the musicians. Debussy in later years described Liszt's pedalling as "like a form of breathing." Debussy and Vidal performed their&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piano_duet" title="Piano duet" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">piano duet</a>&nbsp;arrangement of Liszt's&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faust_Symphony" title="Faust Symphony" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Faust Symphony</a>; allegedly, Liszt fell asleep during this.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-39" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[34]</a></sup></p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">The composer&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camille_Saint-Sa%C3%ABns" title="Camille Saint-Saëns" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Camille Saint-Saëns</a>, an old friend, whom Liszt had once called "the greatest organist in the world", dedicated his&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symphony_No._3_(Saint-Sa%C3%ABns)" title="Symphony No. 3 (Saint-Saëns)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Symphony No. 3 "Organ Symphony"</a>&nbsp;to Liszt; it had premiered in London only a few weeks before the death of its dedicatee.</p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">Liszt died in Bayreuth, Germany, on 31 July 1886, at the age of 74, officially as a result of&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">pneumonia</a>, which he may have contracted during the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bayreuth_Festival" title="Bayreuth Festival" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Bayreuth Festival</a>&nbsp;hosted by his daughter Cosima. Questions have been posed as to whether medical malpractice played a part in his death.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-40" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[35]</a></sup>&nbsp;He was buried on 3 August 1886, in the municipal cemetery of Bayreuth against his wishes.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-41" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; letter-spacing: normal;">There are few, if any, good sources that give an impression of how Liszt really sounded from the 1820s.&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Czerny" title="Carl Czerny" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); At least one also mentions his ability to keep absolute tempo,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-45" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[40]</a></sup>&nbsp;which may be due to his father's insistence that he practice with a metronome.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-46" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[41]</a></sup>&nbsp;His repertoire at this time consisted primarily of pieces in the style of the brilliant Viennese school, such as concertos by&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Nepomuk_Hummel" title="Johann Nepomuk Hummel" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Hummel</a>&nbsp;and works by his former teacher Czerny, and his concerts often included a chance for the boy to display his prowess in improvisation. One of the most detailed descriptions of his playing from this time comes from the winter of 1831–32, during which he was earning a living primarily as a teacher in Paris. Among his pupils was Valerie Boissier, whose mother Caroline kept a careful diary of the lessons. From her we learn that:</p><blockquote style="overflow: hidden; margin-top: 1em; margin-bottom: 1em; padding: 0px 40px; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);"><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit;">M. Liszt's playing contains abandonment, a liberated feeling, but even when it becomes impetuous and energetic in his fortissimo, it is still without harshness and dryness. [...] [He] draws from the piano tones that are purer, mellower and stronger than anyone has been able to do; his touch has an indescribable charm. [...] He is the enemy of affected, stilted, contorted expressions. Most of all, he wants truth in musical sentiment, and so he makes a psychological study of his emotions to convey them as they are. Thus, a strong expression is often followed by a sense of fatigue and dejection, a kind of coldness, because this is the way nature works.</p></blockquote><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">Liszt was sometimes mocked in the press for facial expression and gestures at the piano.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-51" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[n 6]</a></sup>&nbsp;Also noted were the extravagant liberties he could take with the text of a score at this time. Berlioz tells us how Liszt would add cadenzas, tremolos and trills when playing the first movement of Beethoven's&nbsp;<i>Moonlight</i>&nbsp;Sonata, and created a dramatic scene by changing the tempo between Largo and Presto.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-52" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[n 7]</a></sup>&nbsp;In his&nbsp;<i>Baccalaureus letter</i>&nbsp;to George Sand from the beginning of 1837, Liszt admitted that he had done so for the purpose of gaining applause, and promised to follow both the letter and the spirit of a score from then on. It has been debated to what extent he realized his promise, however. By July 1840, the British newspaper&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">The Times</a></i>&nbsp;could still report:</p><blockquote style="overflow: hidden; margin-top: 1em; margin-bottom: 1em; padding: 0px 40px; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);"><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit;">His performance commenced with Handel's Fugue in E minor, which was played by Liszt with an avoidance of everything approaching to meretricious ornament, and indeed scarcely any additions, except a multitude of ingeniously contrived and appropriate harmonies, casting a glow of colour over the beauties of the composition, and infusing into it a spirit which from no other hand it ever before received.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-53" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[46]</a></sup></p></blockquote><h3 style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial; font-weight: bold; margin: 0.3em 0px 0px; overflow: hidden; padding-top: 0.5em; padding-bottom: 0px; border-bottom: 0px; font-size: 1.2em; line-height: 1.6; font-family: sans-serif; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Repertoire">Repertoire</span><span class="mw-editsection" style="user-select: none; font-size: small; font-weight: normal; margin-left: 1em; vertical-align: baseline; line-height: 1em; white-space: nowrap; unicode-bidi: isolate;"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket" style="margin-right: 0.25em; color: rgb(84, 89, 93);">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Franz_Liszt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Repertoire" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket" style="margin-left: 0.25em; color: rgb(84, 89, 93);">]</span></span></h3><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">During his years as a travelling virtuoso, Liszt performed an enormous amount of music throughout Europe,<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-54" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[47]</a></sup>&nbsp;but his core repertoire always centered on his own compositions, paraphrases and transcriptions. Of Liszt's German concerts between 1840 and 1845, the five most frequently played pieces were the&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_galop_chromatique" title="Grand galop chromatique" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Grand galop chromatique</a></i>, Schubert's&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Der_Erlk%C3%B6nig" class="mw-redirect" title="Der Erlkönig" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Erlkönig</a></i>&nbsp;(in Liszt's transcription),&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R%C3%A9miniscences_de_Don_Juan" title="Réminiscences de Don Juan" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Réminiscences de Don Juan</a></i>,&nbsp;<i>Réminiscences de Robert le Diable</i>, and&nbsp;<i>Réminiscences de Lucia di Lammermoor</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-55" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[48]</a></sup>&nbsp;Among the works by other composers were&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Maria_von_Weber" title="Carl Maria von Weber" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Weber</a>'s&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invitation_to_the_Dance_(Weber)" title="Invitation to the Dance (Weber)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Invitation to the Dance</a></i>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Chopin" title="Frédéric Chopin" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Chopin</a>&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mazurkas_(Chopin)" title="Mazurkas (Chopin)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">mazurkas</a>, études by composers like&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ignaz_Moscheles" title="Ignaz Moscheles" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Ignaz Moscheles</a>, Chopin and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_Hiller" title="Ferdinand Hiller" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Ferdinand Hiller</a>, but also major works by Beethoven, Schumann, Weber and Hummel, and from time to time even selections from Bach, Handel and Scarlatti.</p><p style="margin-top: 0.5em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; line-height: inherit; color: rgb(34, 34, 34); font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; letter-spacing: normal;">Most of the concerts at this time were shared with other artists, and as a result Liszt also often accompanied singers, participated in chamber music, or performed works with an orchestra in addition to his own solo part. Frequently played works include Weber's&nbsp;<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Konzertst%C3%BCck_in_F_minor_(Weber)" title="Konzertstück in F minor (Weber)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Konzertstück</a></i>, Beethoven's&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emperor_Concerto" class="mw-redirect" title="Emperor Concerto" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Emperor Concerto</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choral_Fantasy_(Beethoven)" title="Choral Fantasy (Beethoven)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Choral Fantasy</a>, and Liszt's reworking of the&nbsp;<i>Hexameron</i>&nbsp;for piano and orchestra. His chamber music repertoire included&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Nepomuk_Hummel" title="Johann Nepomuk Hummel" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Johann Nepomuk Hummel</a>'s Septet, Beethoven's&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archduke_Trio" class="mw-redirect" title="Archduke Trio" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Archduke Trio</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Violin_Sonata_No._9_(Beethoven)" title="Violin Sonata No. 9 (Beethoven)" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Kreutzer Sonata</a>, and a large selection of songs by composers like&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gioachino_Rossini" title="Gioachino Rossini" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Gioachino Rossini</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaetano_Donizetti" title="Gaetano Donizetti" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Gaetano Donizetti</a>, Beethoven and especially&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Schubert" title="Franz Schubert" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Franz Schubert</a>. At some concerts, Liszt could not find musicians to share the program with, and consequently was among the first to give solo piano recitals in the modern sense of the word. The term was coined by the publisher Frederick Beale, who suggested it for Liszt's concert at the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanover_Square_Rooms" title="Hanover Square Rooms" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">Hanover Square Rooms</a>&nbsp;in London on 9 June 1840,<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-56" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[49]</a></sup>&nbsp;even though Liszt had given concerts all by himself already by March 1839.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference" style="line-height: 1; unicode-bidi: isolate; white-space: nowrap; font-size: 11.2px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Liszt#cite_note-57" style="color: rgb(11, 0, 128); background-image: none; background-position: initial; background-size: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial;">[50]</a></sup></p>
